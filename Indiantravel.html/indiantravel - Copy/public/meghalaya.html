About Meghalaya

Meghalaya, meaning "Abode of Clouds," is a breathtaking state in Northeast India known for its stunning natural beauty, unique living root bridges, and pristine waterfalls. Often called the "Scotland of the East" due to its rolling hills and green landscapes, Meghalaya offers a perfect escape for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

The state is home to the Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo tribes, each with their own distinct culture and traditions. Meghalaya's unique geography includes deep gorges, limestone caves, and some of the heaviest rainfall areas in the world, creating a lush, green paradise that's unlike anywhere else in India.

Culture & Heritage

Meghalaya is home to three main tribal groups - Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo - each with their own distinct culture, language, and traditions. The state has a matrilineal society where property and family names are passed through the female line.

The tribes have a deep connection with nature and celebrate various festivals that reflect their agricultural lifestyle and spiritual beliefs. Traditional music, dance, and handicrafts are integral parts of Meghalaya's cultural heritage.

Meghalaya Cuisine

Meghalaya's cuisine reflects its tribal heritage and uses locally available ingredients. Rice, meat, and vegetables form the staple diet, with unique preparations that highlight the region's agricultural bounty.

From the famous Jadoh (rice and meat) to various pork preparations, Meghalaya offers a distinctive culinary experience that's both flavorful and unique.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Meghalaya experience

Best Time to Visit

September to May offers pleasant weather. Monsoon season brings heavy rains but lush greenery.

Getting Around

Flights to Shillong, then buses/taxis. Roads can be winding. Carry warm clothes for higher altitudes.

Local Etiquette

Respect tribal customs. Photography may require permission. Learn basic greetings in local languages.
