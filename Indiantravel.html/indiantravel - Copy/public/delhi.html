About Delhi

Delhi, India's capital city, is a fascinating blend of ancient history and modern dynamism. As the political, cultural, and commercial heart of India, Delhi offers visitors an unparalleled journey through time - from the majestic Mughal monuments to contemporary urban landscapes.

The city has served as the capital of various empires and dynasties, each leaving their architectural imprint. From the magnificent Red Fort built by Emperor Shah Jahan to the intricate Qutub Minar, Delhi's historical monuments tell stories of India's glorious past.

Must-Visit Attractions

Discover the iconic landmarks that make Delhi unforgettable

Red Fort

A magnificent 17th-century fortress built by Emperor Shah Jahan, symbolizing Mughal architectural grandeur.

2-3 hours UNESCO Site
Qutub Minar

The world's tallest brick minaret, showcasing intricate Islamic architecture and ancient engineering.

1-2 hours UNESCO Site
India Gate

A war memorial honoring Indian soldiers, surrounded by lush gardens and serving as a popular picnic spot.

1 hour Free Entry
Lotus Temple

A Bahá'í House of Worship known for its stunning lotus-shaped architecture and serene atmosphere.

1-2 hours Free Entry
Akshardham Temple

A magnificent Hindu temple complex featuring intricate carvings, musical fountains, and cultural exhibits.

3-4 hours Paid Entry
Chandni Chowk

Delhi's oldest and busiest market, offering authentic street food, spices, and traditional crafts.

2-3 hours Shopping

Culture & Heritage

Delhi's culture is a rich tapestry woven from diverse influences - Mughal, British, and indigenous traditions. The city's festivals reflect this diversity, from Diwali celebrations to Eid festivities.

The city's museums and cultural centers offer insights into India's freedom struggle, ancient civilizations, and contemporary art scene.

Delhi Cuisine

Delhi's food scene is a culinary journey through India's diverse flavors. From street food to fine dining, the city offers an incredible variety of tastes.

Don't miss trying authentic Delhi dishes like butter chicken, chole bhature, paratha, and the famous Delhi chaat.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Delhi experience

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers pleasant weather. Avoid summer months (April-June) due to extreme heat.

Getting Around

Use Metro for efficient travel. Ola/Uber for short distances. Prepaid taxis for airport transfers.

Local Etiquette

Dress modestly at religious sites. Remove shoes when entering temples. Bargain at markets politely.
