About Kerala

Kerala, often called "God's Own Country," is a tropical paradise located on India's southwestern coast. Known for its serene backwaters, pristine beaches, lush green landscapes, and rich cultural heritage, Kerala offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and traditional charm.

The state is famous for its unique geography - from the Arabian Sea coastline to the Western Ghats mountains, creating diverse ecosystems that support everything from spice plantations to wildlife sanctuaries. Ayurvedic treatments, Kathakali dance performances, and houseboat cruises on the backwaters are among Kerala's most cherished experiences.

Must-Visit Attractions

Discover the natural wonders and cultural treasures of Kerala

Alleppey Backwaters

Experience a serene houseboat cruise through Kerala's famous backwater network, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

1-2 days UNESCO Site
Munnar Tea Gardens

Rolling hills covered with tea plantations, misty mountains, and wildlife in this scenic hill station.

2-3 days Nature

Three beautiful crescent-shaped beaches known for their golden sands, clear waters, and Ayurvedic treatments.

A wildlife sanctuary famous for its elephant herds, boat safaris, and biodiversity in the Western Ghats.

1 day Wildlife

Iconic cantilevered fishing nets along the waterfront, a unique blend of Chinese and local fishing techniques.

Culture & Heritage

Kerala's culture is deeply rooted in tradition, with influences from various civilizations. The state is known for its performing arts like Kathakali, Kalaripayattu (martial arts), and traditional temple festivals.

Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine, originated in Kerala and remains a cornerstone of the state's wellness tourism.

Kerala Cuisine

Kerala cuisine is renowned for its use of coconut, spices, and seafood. The food is typically served on banana leaves and emphasizes fresh, local ingredients.

From spicy curries to sweet desserts, Kerala offers a culinary journey that's both flavorful and healthy.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Kerala experience

Best Time to Visit

September to March offers pleasant weather. Monsoon season (June-August) brings lush greenery but heavy rains.

Getting Around

Houseboats for backwaters, taxis for hill stations, and buses for local travel. Domestic flights connect major cities.

Local Etiquette

Dress modestly at temples. Try local food with respect. Learn basic Malayalam greetings for warm interactions.
