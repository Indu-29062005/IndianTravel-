About Karnataka

Karnataka, located on India's southwestern coast, is a state rich in history, culture, and natural diversity. From the bustling IT capital of Bengaluru to ancient temple towns, Karnataka offers a perfect blend of tradition and modernity. The state is home to some of India's most magnificent temples, wildlife sanctuaries, and coffee plantations.

Known for its contributions to Indian classical music, dance, literature, and cuisine, Karnataka has been a center of learning and culture for centuries. The state's diverse geography includes coastal regions, deciduous forests, and the Western Ghats, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Must-Visit Attractions

Discover Karnataka's temples, wildlife, and cultural treasures

Mysore Palace

A magnificent Indo-Saracenic palace known for its stunning architecture and evening light show.

2-3 hours Royal Palace
Hampi

Ancient ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning temples and boulders.

1-2 days UNESCO Site
Coorg (Kodagu)

A scenic hill station famous for coffee plantations, waterfalls, and colonial-era bungalows.

2-3 days Hill Station
Badami Caves

Ancient rock-cut cave temples showcasing Chalukya architecture and intricate carvings from the 6th century.

2-3 hours Historical
Bandipur National Park

A wildlife sanctuary famous for tigers, elephants, and diverse flora in the Western Ghats.

1 day Wildlife
Gokarna Beach

A peaceful beach town known for its pristine beaches, temples, and laid-back atmosphere.

1-2 days Beach

Culture & Heritage

Karnataka has a rich cultural heritage with contributions to Indian classical music (Carnatic), dance forms like Bharatanatyam, and literature. The state is home to numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites and ancient temple architecture.

Festivals like Dassara, Ugadi, and Karaga reflect the state's diverse cultural traditions. Karnataka has also been a center for Jainism, Buddhism, and various Hindu traditions.

Karnataka Cuisine

Karnataka cuisine is diverse and flavorful, featuring dishes from coastal, mountain, and plains regions. Rice, coconut, and spices form the base of many traditional dishes.

From the famous Bisi Bele Bath to coastal seafood curries, Karnataka offers a culinary journey that's both delicious and varied.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Karnataka experience

Best Time to Visit

October to May offers pleasant weather. Monsoon season (June-September) brings greenery but heavy rains.

Getting Around

Excellent rail and road connectivity. Domestic flights available. Buses and taxis for local travel.

Local Etiquette

Remove shoes at temples. Dress modestly at religious sites. Learn basic Kannada greetings for warm interactions.
